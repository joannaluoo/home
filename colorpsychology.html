Color Psychology

Different colors can evoke different feelings in people. Of course a color doesn’t always evoke the same feeling in different people, as personal experiences influence your opinion on a color, but colors often evoke similar feelings in people, which is how brands use colors to attract their audience. You can apply color psychology in art and design by choosing specific colors for your piece that will set a specific mood for it, and evoke specific feelings in the person looking at it.

What Colors Represent Which Emotions?

Red

Red provokes the strongest feelings in Color Psychology. Red can represent passion and love, as well as power and anger. It is also known to represent danger, excitement, aggression, and dominance.

Orange

Orange is often described as bright, and happy, and is a color that attracts attention. Orange is linked to things such as spirituality and fall.

Yellow

Yellow is a color that is often associated with warmth, happiness, energy, attention, and brightness. Yellow can also cause feelings of frustration and is tiring on the eyes.

Green

Green usually reminds people of nature safety, and luck, but can also represent envy. It also is linked to calmness, motivation, and optimism.

Blue

Blue can represent Stability, calmness, sadness, and productivity. It can also be sincere, intense, or inspiring.

Purple

Purple most commonly evokes feelings of wealth, royalty, mystery, and imagination. It can also mean wisdom, bravery, spirituality, and can be thought of as unique and exotic.

Pink

Pink often reminds people of love, nurturing, calmness, and kindness. Pink also can mean joy, creativity, and femininity, and can be refreshing and childish.

Brown

Brown Evokes feelings of nature, strength, isolation, and security. Brown reminds people of things like resilience and comfort. It can also be associated with negative emotions, as most dark colors are.

White

White is often thought to represent emptiness, innocence, cleanliness, and peacefulness. White can also be cold, isolated, simplistic, pure, and fresh.

Black

Black is usually considered mysterious, powerful, bold, and unhappy. Black is often associated with fanciness and wealth, as well as death, evil, fear, anger, and sadness.
